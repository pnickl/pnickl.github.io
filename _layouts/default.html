Peter Nickl

Since 2021, I am a research assistant with Emtiyaz Khan at the Approximate Bayesian Inference Team, RIKEN Center for AI Project in Tokyo. I work on understanding the sensitivity of machine learning models to data perturbation as well as deep learning with probabilistic methods. I was part of the winning team of the NeurIPS 2021 challenge Approximate Inference in Bayesian Deep Learning and helped organizing the ACML 2022 Continual Lifelong Learning Workshop.

I have studied computational engineering, mechanical engineering and economics at TU Darmstadt. I did my thesis at the Intelligent Autonomous Systems Lab headed by Prof. Jan Peters on approximate inference algorithms for an infinite mixture of local linear regression models with applications to robot control. In 2020, I was part of the spin-off team Telekinesis AI that acquired an EIC transition grant on «Visual Robot Programming».

During my studies, I spent two years as a language and exchange student at Beijing Institute of Technology and Tongji University. I interned at Bosch Rexroth and Continental Automotive Systems in Shanghai.
